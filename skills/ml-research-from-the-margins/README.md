# Doing ML Research from the Margins

**Skills session · Deep Learning Indaba 2026**

**Session leaders:** Steven Kolawole (CMU LTI · AWS Bedrock · ML Collective Africa) and
Ifihanagbara Olusheye (Wits University · ML Collective Africa)

## Summary

Doing frontier ML research from a constrained context — no lab, no cluster, no
network — feels impossible, but the people who break through all make a small
number of identifiable moves. This session lays out those moves as a practical,
navigable map: finding and approaching mentors, scoping research that's tractable
without massive compute, reading the literature efficiently, and writing and
positioning work for top venues. The goal isn't inspiration — it's to leave you
with a clearer map than we had.

## What's covered

1. **Framing** — what the path looks like in retrospect; cumulative advantage and
   the four essential moves (find community early, make yourself a worthwhile bet,
   scope carefully, build momentum from small wins).
2. **Finding mentors** — the outreach funnel, where to find accessible senior
   researchers, and the anatomy of a cold email that gets a reply.
3. **Scoping research** — the three-property tractability filter, estimating your
   compute bill before you start, and turning your local context into an asset.
4. **Reading the literature** — Keshav's three-pass method and going from zero to
   frontier-aware in a week, while building reputation in the open.
5. **Top venue positioning** — how peer review actually works, framing a
   contribution with command, rebuttals, and using OpenReview as a free advisor.
